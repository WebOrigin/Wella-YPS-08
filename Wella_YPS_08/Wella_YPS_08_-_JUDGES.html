Danny Pato is a Director of D&M Hair Design in Ponsonby, and has worked with a number of leading salons in the Auckland region throughout his career. With an accomplished background including winning numerous regional and national awards, Danny also thrives on doing editorial and fashion work for magazines and the fashion industry. A highlight from 2007 for Danny, was when he and his team from D&M worked backstage on the Huffer Show at ANZFW. Danny was thrilled to be a part of the show and enjoyed seeing his team take their creativity to another level. Danny encourages younger staff to look at a variety of creative industries to ensure they continue to grow creatively and personally.

Shar from Sutherland Todd Hairdressing in Christchurch has a record number of achievements under her belt.  She has twice been a finalist for the Sydney Hair Expo New Zealand Hairdresser of the Year as well as winning numerous competition awards both regionally and nationally.  Shar has a reputation for training to the highest level which has seen her lead many winning YPS stylists over the years.  As a strong supporter of YPS and the opportunities the event provides the youth of our industry, Shar believes passion and creativity unleashed is potential fulfilled. 

Karl Radel began his hairdressing career 18 years ago, today he and his wife Danelle own Klone Hair in Dunedin which celebrates nine years in business. Karl has a passion for competition work and has achieved high levels of success, winning over 50 places in national hairdressing competitions including Supreme Hairdresser of the Year. He has passed this winning competition experience through to his team members, having trained two previous Wella YPS national winners. Karl is also an accomplished NZARH Judge having judged throughout the country for over 10 years. He is extremely passionate about the hairdressing industry as it is continually evolving and feels it is both inspiring and challenging.

Bronwyn Illingworth began her career in Dunedin before moving to the UK where she worked with TONI&GUY for 10 years, becoming an International Artistic Director and Show Director. With an enthusiasm and passion for hairdressing that is relentless – Bronwyn has had an incredible career - some of her highlights being London fashion week, Educational videos for TONI&GUY International, Fashion Rocks in Monaco, and an expert for the TV series “Ten Years Younger in Ten Days”. Her work has been published in and on the covers of fashion and hair magazines nationally and internationally but Bronwyn’s true passion is educational show work that has taken her all over the world and in front of audiences up to 6,000 – she believes in giving back to the industry. Bronwyn is now Managing Educational Director for TONI&GUY New Zealand.

Jaimee Smith is the Director of Zaibatsu Hairart, a successful Dunedin based business that she began from a young age. Jaimee has a strong passion for hairdressing, especially competition judging as it provides the opportunity to meet a number of talented and creative people from the industry. Jaimee has had a number of exciting highlights throughout her career; she has won numerous regional and national hair awards and has had a variety of magazine features. Part of Jaimee’s enthusiasm for the hairdressing industry is due to the inspirational and motivated people she has had the pleasure in working alongside throughout her career, people such as Michelle Ogden and Shar Sutherland-Todd from earlier years, to the current team at Zaibatsu, who share her own enthusiasm and excitement.

Michael Beel is the Creative Director of Buoy Hairdressing in Wellington and has been hairdressing since 1997.Since that time he has grown into one of New Zealand's leading educators and has also taught in Asia and India with Wella Professionals. Michael has also won regional and national events, been a finalist for NZ Hairdresser of the year both here in New Zealand and in Australia at Hair Expo. His work is based on merging creativity with the balance of precision cutting. Michael believes in celebrating womanhood and creative hairdressing that embellishes the essence of femininity.
